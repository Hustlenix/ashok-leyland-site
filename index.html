Our Story

A Legacy of Moving Nations

Incorporated in 1948 as Ashok Motors Limited, our journey began by assembling Austin cars in Ennore, Chennai. A strategic partnership with British Leyland transformed us into Ashok Leyland — a name that would become synonymous with commercial vehicle excellence in India and beyond.

As the flagship of the Hinduja Group, we have pioneered innovation for over 75 years: India's first 13-ton truck, first multi-axle vehicle, first CNG bus, and first hybrid electric vehicle. Today, we are the second-largest CV manufacturer in India and the fourth-largest bus manufacturer in the world.

Our philosophy — "Koi Manzil Door Nahin" (No Dream Too Far) — drives our commitment to engineering excellence, sustainability, and global mobility solutions.

"Our strategic partnership with CALB is a significant step towards creating a localised battery supply chain in India to accelerate adoption of electric vehicles. A Global Centre of Excellence will be created to serve as a hub for research and development."
— Shenu Agarwal, MD & CEO, Ashok Leyland

Our Journey

75+ Years of Breaking Barriers

From assembling cars in 1948 to pioneering electric mobility in 2026 — every milestone reflects our relentless pursuit of engineering excellence.

1948

The Beginning

Incorporated as Ashok Motors Limited in Chennai. Began assembling Austin cars, laying the foundation for India's automotive journey.

1980

Trucking Revolution

Inaugurated Hosur plant. Launched India's first 13-ton truck 'Tusker' and first multi-axle truck 'Taurus' — redefining freight transport.

1997

Green Pioneer

Launched India's first CNG-powered bus and the legendary 'Stallion' all-terrain logistics vehicle, still the backbone of Indian defence logistics.

2002

Hybrid Breakthrough

Developed India's first Hybrid Electric Vehicle, foreshadowing the company's eventual dominance in zero-emission mobility.

2010s

Global Expansion

Strategic JVs with Continental AG, Alteams, and John Deere. Acquired Optare PLC (UK), laying groundwork for Switch Mobility. Forged global supply chains.

2026

Historic Peak

Record revenue of ₹56,947 Cr. Switch Mobility turns profitable with 1,530 e-buses. ₹5,000 Cr battery ecosystem announced. 220,437 vehicles sold.
